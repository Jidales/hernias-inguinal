What Every Parent Should Know About Hernias

An inguinal hernia appears as a bulge in your child’s groin. It happens when a part of the intestine or ovary (in girls) slips through a weak spot in the abdominal wall. It may look small, but it can become dangerous quickly.

If left untreated, it can lead to sudden pain, vomiting, or tissue damage (strangulation). Surgery is the only permanent solution — and today, it's safer than ever.
